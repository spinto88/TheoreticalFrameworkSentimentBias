              Outlet Bias

Latent score for each media outlet. The sign of z has no standalone meaning — what matters is its product with the subject's discrimination parameter a: if z·a > 0 the outlet reinforces positive sentiment toward that subject; if z·a < 0 it reinforces negative sentiment.

          

        


        

          

            

              Subject Discrimination
              a
            

            

              
              
            

          

          
Discrimination parameter for each subject. Like z, the sign of a is only meaningful in combination with an outlet's score: the product z·a determines the direction of reinforcement — positive means the outlet tends to cover the subject favourably, negative means unfavourably. Larger absolute values of a amplify this effect.

          

        


        

          

            

              Subject Baseline
              b
            

            

              
              
            

          

          
Baseline sentiment for each subject, independent of which outlet covers it. A positive b indicates a general tendency across all outlets to mention this subject positively; a negative b indicates an overall negative tendency.
